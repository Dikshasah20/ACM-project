ARTICLE I: ACM



ACM stands for the Association for Computing Machinery. It is a professional organization that focuses on computing as a science and a profession. ACM was founded in 1947 and is one of the world's largest and most prestigious computing societies. ACM provides a platform for computer scientists, researchers, and professionals to exchange ideas, share research findings, and stay updated on the latest developments in the field of computing. The organization publishes numerous journals, magazines, and conference proceedings covering a wide range of topics within computer science.

ARTICLE V: CHAPTER SPONSOR


An ACM (Association for Computing Machinery) chapter sponsor is an individual or organization that provides support, guidance, and resources to an ACM student chapter at a particular academic institution. The sponsor plays a crucial role in fostering the growth and success of the ACM chapter by offering financial assistance, mentorship, and access to industry connections. Here are some key aspects of an ACM chapter sponsor: Financial Support: Sponsors often provide funding to help the ACM chapter organize events, conferences, workshops, and other activities. This financial support can cover expenses such as venue rentals, catering, promotional materials, and guest speaker fees. Mentorship and Guidance: Sponsors may offer mentorship to the chapter members, helping them navigate their academic and professional journeys in the field of computing. This guidance can include advice on career paths, industry trends, and educational opportunities. Resource Allocation: Sponsors may assist in obtaining resources such as software licenses, hardware, or other tools that are essential for the chapter's activities and projects. Industry Connections: A sponsor can facilitate connections between the ACM chapter and industry professionals, creating opportunities for students to network, learn about job opportunities, and gain insights into the practical applications of computing. Guest Speakers and Workshops: Sponsors may help coordinate guest speakers or experts to conduct workshops, seminars, or lectures for the ACM chapter. This enriches the educational experience for chapter members and exposes them to real-world perspectives.


ARTICLE VI: MEETINGS


The Chapter shall hold meetings only in places that are open and accessible to all members of the Association. The Annual Election meeting should be held at the last meeting of the semester. At this meeting, the Treasurer will present the required reports. Also, the election of officers shall be held. Written notices of all meetings shall be distributed to all members at least one week prior to any meeting.


ARTICLE VII: DISSOLUTION OF THE CHAPTER


Dissolution of this Chapter by consent of the members shall consist of unanimous agreement of all its officers together with a majority vote at a meeting which has been publicized in advance to all members of the Chapter for the purpose of taking this vote. Should this Chapter be dissolved, its assets and liabilities shall be transferred to ACM and shall be supervised by the ACM Finance Director. Funds given to the chapter from the University shall be returned to the University
